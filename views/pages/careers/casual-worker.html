A casual worker

A manager of one of the homes within My Choice called me at 6 pm on Wednesday evening and asked if I was available to work a shift the following day. Itâ€™s quite common to be phoned the day before or even on the morning of the shift that needs to be covered. They needed a Casual Worker to cover a member of staff who had called in sick for their shift the following day.

I agreed to work the whole shift and to start at 10am on the Thursday morning to finish Friday around 10am. The manager also spoke to me about my availability for later on in the month to help cover shifts as some staff had annual leave and also need to attend training. This is great as I now have 9 shifts booked this month.

I arrived at 10am as arranged and was given a handover by the staff on shift. I was informed that I needed to take a specific young person to a dentist appointment during the shift and to prepare lunch and dinner for all at the home, before I take one of the young people to the cinema for an evening activity.

During the day it is also my responsibility to write up in the weekly log what has happened today, from start to finish, for record purposes.

I am also required to do the following;

  • 1:1 working with all the young people
  • Preparing meals for the home
  • Undertaking all domestic responsibilities within the home.
  • Taking young people to appointments
  • Support in the education setting if required

It is really busy at the home and the day can be so varied and we all work as a team providing a nurturing and learning environment for the young people.

During my shifts there is always a chance that I may be asked to work at one of the other homes, but I am used to this as I have worked in all homes now as a Casual Worker.

At the end of each day we settle the young people for a quiet evening and this helps with their bed time routines. We are available throughout the day and night, which is why we sleep in, should the young people need support or guidance.
