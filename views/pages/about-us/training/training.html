Objectives

The objectives of My Choice is to ensure young people are looked after, cared for and receive the services they need by competent staff who are trained to meet their needs. We give our staff the opportunity to receive training to broaden their skills and enable a better understanding of young people's needs and the purpose of the home and school.

The first stage of training & development is obtained by attending the Head Office Induction Day. This is an opportunity for new staff to gain an understanding of the recording systems, policies & procedures required to fulfil the role of a Residential Support Worker. Child protection, medication and fire awareness is also discussed. The new staff member will then begin the Induction Programme which lasts 6 months. This induction has been developed from the CWDC (Children's Workforce Development Council) Standards. It incorporates key areas for development and knowledge within residential care.

Staff work through the induction by attending training sessions, working with other colleagues and respective Line Managers. The induction programme cross-references into the Level 3 Diploma in Children & Young People's Workforce qualification and Codes of Practice for social care workers from the GSCC (General Social Care Council).

My Choice offers a really great training opportunity, lots of variety and there is always something new to learn. My Choice support staff through the Diploma, promote high standards and in the home I am based in, the team work so well together, have fun and do the best we can for the young people.
We continue to develop training and support staff to work through their Personal Development Portfolio.
